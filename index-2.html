About the Conference Annual Africa Conference on Social Entrepreneurship (AACOSE)

The Annual Africa Conference on Social Entrepreneurship (AACOSE) is an initiative set in place to foster the interaction between the key actors who contribute to creating favourable conditions for the growth of sustainable social enterprises in Africa Learn More

Meet our Conference Speakers

Pape Samb

Ashoka Global Vice President & Executive Director for Africa

Pape Samb

Ashoka Global Vice President & Executive Director for Africa
Pape has twenty years of leadership experience, with an established record of strategic planning, youth and women entrepreneurship, program development and management, content management, fundraising, training and facilitation. His main role at Ashka is to create the social demand for a new framework that fosters changemaking mindsets in the people and communities in Africa, and address the continental challenges through fellows’ engagement, co-creation with companies, youth venture, and empathy.

Conference Thematic Focus

Climate change resilience

Climate change resilience

Climate change is creating devastating effects on the lives of many people and across the sectors of society. Innovative solutions for building resilience to climate change are required.
Social Innovation

Social Innovation

In the face of global challenges, the need for institutional transformation in academia is important, especially to provide practical training for social innovators and develop their innovative and transformative teaching. They need to embrace pedagogies that transform the mindset of the learners towards social innovation, promote an educational approach that takes the learners to innovate to communities
Technology for Humanity

Technology for Humanity

This new era of rapid change, complexity, and uncertainty calls for us to be at the forefront looking at innovation insights that create a just and humane future for us all. Some of the issues to be addressed are; 
a. The impact of COVID 19 and the digital divide
b. Tech innovations for resilient healthcare and education systems (especially Higher
Education)
c. Data protection, digital ethics, and fake news; 
d. Tech innovations supporting livelihoods especially for youth and women.
Scaling for social impact

Scaling for social impact

Sharing the experiences, technique, and practice so scaling the most impact solutions in Africa. There is fundamental to create a support system and develop the capacity of social entrepreneurs to scale their impact.
Bio-cultural-economic drivers

Bio-cultural-economic drivers

The potential for the nourishment of social entrepreneurship is immense. Innovative enterprises, technologies, and governance protocols are just starting to tap the relationship between the science of bioavailable nutrients (in both humans and soils) and the forces that drive local ingenuity worldwide. This relationship produces the cycles of nourishment—as vitality circulates and increases between people and the land can become social, environmental, and economic powerhouses.
Social entrepreneurship legal framework

Social entrepreneurship legal framework

African countries need to develop the legal framework for the growth of social enterprises. There are some emerging initiatives but a lot is still to be done.
